    Hi-Speed (Basics)

    This page is for brief introduction to differnet Hi-Speed modes. Please understand that I will not go into indepth details of how each Hi-Speeds work.

    If you are not familiar with different Hi-Speed modes and how to use them, please head over to IIDX.org and review [Hi-Speed Options(https://iidx.org/beginner/hispeed) and Floating Hi-Speed

    For specific terminologies used on this page, refer to Terminology section.

    Different Hi-Speed modes

    Currently, IIDX has 3 different Hi-Speed modes that you can choose from: - Normal Hi-Speed - Classic Hi-Speed - Floating Hi-Speed

    During song select, you can select the option between Normal Hi-Speed and Classic Hi-Speed. You can turn on floating in-game by holding down START + pressing EFFECT.

    So the two ways the Hi-Speeds will work are: - Normal Hi-Speed ↔ Floating Hi-Speed - Classic Hi-Speed ↔ Floating Hi-Speed

    As mentioned in the terminology section, the Floating Hi-Speed (FHS) works differently when the SUDDEN+/LIFT is on or not.

    If you do not use any of the lane cover options, the Green Number (GN) is not locked in. Thus, floating (act of holding down Start then turning the turntable) will cause the GN to change. (To do: Check to see if the behavior is same in terms of still being able to "lock-in" GN).

    If you use any of the lane cover options, FHS will lock in your GN. What this means is that, the game will save your GN once the FHS is turned on. Any floats when lane cover will set your GN to the 'saved' value.

    It is highly recommended, at the very lease, to use SUDDEN+ even if it only covers only little part of the screen. This is because there's more advantages of having SUDDEN+ on for setting GN as well as floating through soflan songs.

    FHS usage is (99%) Mandatory due to Floating

    As you can see on IIDX.org and various other sites, it is almost mandatory (highly, HIGHLY recommended) to use FHS. Soflan charts is one of the reasons.

    As mentioned above, FHS will remember the 'GN' you save when you turn on. The advantage of this is that you can float after a BPM change and it will 'reset' your GN.

    For example, lets say a song slowed down going from 200 BPM to 100 BPM. This will double your GN in-game, and the notes will stay on the screen longer than usual (with narrower note spacing). If you float during this BPM change, the game will 'reset' the GN to the saved value, thus reverting back to your normal GN.

    Floating (and the ideas behind the usage) will be covered more indepth in Floating (Beginner) and Floating (Advanced).

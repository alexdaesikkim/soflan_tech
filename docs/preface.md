# Preface

## Why have a new website for notes?
While there are plenty of information out there on various websites (as well as youtube videos on individual songs), there were couple of things that I did not like.

First, any information post IIDX Bistrover (BPL Season 1) seems to be either uncovered or missing in documentations. This was a time period when, due to the physical changes of where start button is, various new strategies were being experimented on. There were two key important soflan techniques that became a main stay, but I have never seen this being discussed elsewhere.

Second, the uncovered information above is spread out through various videos. While there are few youtube videos that shows what they do for each song, they do not discuss in detail on how/why the technique works.

The overall goal of this website is to share as much information as possible on techniques and theories. Using this information, I want players to apply the techniques and experiment to find what is best 'for them'. But...

## Why not just post examples to copy from?
I believe that, for the most part, there are various ways to tackle a song. There might be the most 'optimal' way that may seem great for some, but ridiculous to execute for others.

I've noticed over the years that best soflan players find what is best for their playstyle (whether it be physical style or reading style).

## But...... why have all these complicated explanations? Why not just float at every speed change?
Great point, and I can't really disagree with that. IIDX is already frustrating enough as is, and learning additional stuff just to tackle soflan is a chore.

The reason why... If there is a way to float through a song, I wanted to see if there is a way to minimize the EX points lost from doing the floating. I wanted to see if there is a way to float so that I do not drop any combo. I always wanted to explore the 'what if' to take myself to the next level with soflan.

I understand that most players will neglect information posted here, and that's fine. I'm here for the small percentage of players who may find this information interesting, and trying to help them in their soflan journey.
